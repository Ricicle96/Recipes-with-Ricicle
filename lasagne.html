Lasagne al Forno

Lasagne al forno is the ultimate comfort food: slow-simmered tomato sauce, rich beef ragù, silky béchamel and tender pasta sheets baked together until bubbling and golden. It’s a classic Italian dish that feels special without being complicated — just a few simple steps, layered with a little patience.

This version keeps things approachable and flavour-forward, with a smooth tomato base and a béchamel that turns beautifully creamy in the oven. It’s perfect for feeding a crowd, making ahead, and (arguably) even better the next day.

Method

Basic Tomato Sauce

  1. Heat the oil in a pan and add the vegetables and bay leaf.
  2. Gently cook for 10 to 15 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in the tomatoes and 150ml of water.
  4. Simmer, very gently, stirring occasionaly to ensure the sauce isn't sticking to the pan, for 30 minutes until the sauce has reduced by two-thirds and is thicker.*
  5. Optional, but not neccesary, you can use an immersion blender to blitz the sauce and make it smoother.

*If you like your sauce a little thinner, add a splash more water.

Bechamel

  1. Bring the milk to a boil in a small saucepan with the onion, bay leaf and cloves. Turn off the heat and leave to infuse for 20 minutes. (This infusion process is optional, but adds a huge depth of flavour to the sauce)
  2. Melt the butter in another saucepan, then add the plain flour. Stir continuosly until a paste forms - this is your basic roux. Continue cooking for 1-2 minutes until the raw flour smell disipates.
  3. Remove the onion, bay leaf and cloves from your milk with a slotted spoon and discard. Add the infused milk to your roux gradually, stirring as you go, making sure the milk is fully combined with the roux before adding more, until you have completely used all the milk and your sauce is smooth.*
  4. Cook the sauce for 5-10 minutes, stirring continuosly, until the sauce has thickened. Season to taste with freshly ground salt and white pepper.

*If your sauce came out a bit on the lumpier side, grab a fine mesh sieve and pass the sauce through it into a bowl to remove the lumps.

Assemble and Bake

  1. To make the meat sauce, heat 2 tbsp of olive oil in a frying pan and cook your 750g minced beef for around 10 minutes until browned. Don't forget to season your beef with freshly ground salt and black pepper.
  2. Pour over around half of the Basic Tomato Sauce we made, along with your beef stock and grate your nutmeg directly into it.*
  3. Bring this mixture to a boil, then turn it down to simmer with a lid for 30 minutes, until the sauce looks rich.
  4. Pre-heat your oven to 180C/160C fan/gas mark 4 and lightly oil an ovenproof dish, roughly 30cm x 20cm in size.
  5. Once your meat sauce is finished simmering, spoon approximately a third into the dish, then cover with some of the fresh lasagne sheets.
  6. Drizzle over enough of your bechamel to cover the lasagne sheets.
  7. Repeat the process in step 6 until you have 3 layers of pasta. Cover the top layer with bechamel, making sure you can't see any pasta poking through.
  8. Scatter your grated mozzarella over the top layer of bechamel.
  9. Bake in the oven for 45 minutes, until the top is bubbling and lightly browned.
